Sign constituents

Each sign may be specified for one or more of the following parameters, which together make up the sign. If a sign is not specified for a certain parameter, changes in that parameter does not alter its lexical meaning (though it may have grammatical significance).

Handshape
Configuration of the fingers, e.g. fist, flat hand, thumbs-up, etc.
Hand orientation
Direction the fingers and palm are pointing. When not specified, any orientation is allowed.
Hand constellation
Whether the hands are near, touching, clasped, etc. Never specified in 1-handed signs. If not specified in a 2-handed sign, the hands are apart by default.
Hand symmetry
In 2-handed signs, whether the hands mirror each other. Never specified in 1-handed signs. If not specified in a 2-handed sign, the hands are asymmetric and their location and handshape must be specified.
Location
Location where the sign is articulated.
Movement
The sign's movement.
Non-manuals
Articulations that do not involve the hand. If not specified, the facial expression and body posture is neutral by default.

Location inventory

The following locations may appear in signs. The hamlrat black square indicates the left or right side of a body part; for example, hamlrat,hamhead indicates left of head and hamhead,hamlrat indicates right of head. The hamlrbeside white square indicates a location next to the left or right of a body part.

    Head and face

  • hamhead Head
  • hamforehead, hamforehead,hamlrbeside Forehead
  • hameyes Eyes
  • hamear Ear
  • hamnose Nose
  • hamlrat,hamlips hamlips, hamlips,hamlrat Lips
  • hamchin Chin
  • hamunderchin Below chin

    Body and other

  • hamneutralspace Neutral space
  • hamneck, hamneck,hamlrat Neck
  • hamlrat,hamshoulders Shoulders
  • hamupperarm Upper arm
  • hamlowerarm Lower arm
  • hamwristback Back of wrist
  • hamlrat,hamchest hamchest Chest
  • hamstomach Stomach
