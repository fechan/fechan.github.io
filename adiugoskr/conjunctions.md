## fir and on - Complement and predicative *'and'*
### fir: complement cumulative conjunction
Complement cumulative conjunction **fir** *'and'* comes between all constituents
that act as complements, such as noun phrases, as well as complementizer phrases,
and prepositional phrases that do not modify nouns.

<div class="gloss">
  Lowí fir njáu fir rofá yi sjinen ón.<br/>
  Lowí fir njáu fir rofá yi sjinen ón<br/>
  snake and cat and dog to pub walk\AN<br/>
  ! 'A snake, a cat, and a dog walk into a pub.'
</div>

In the following example, the two complementizer phrases
**tún rrofá jár** *'that the dog is eating'* and
**tún njáu vól** *'that the cat is drinking'* are both complements to the verb
**see** *'jadé'*, so they are coordinated by fir.

<div class="gloss">
  Tún rrofá jár fir tún njáu vól jadé.<br/>
  tún rrofá jár fir tún njáu vól ja-dé<br/>
  SEN dog eat\AN and SEN cat drink\AN see-1>3<br/>
  ! 'I see that the dog is eating and the cat is drinking.'
</div>

### on: predicative cumulative conjunction
The predicative cumulative conjunction **on** *'and.PRED'* comes between all
constituents that act as predicates, such as verb phrases, adjective phrases, as
well as complementizer phrases and prepositional phrases that modify nouns.

In the following example, the two complementizer phrases are **not** verbal
complements, but instead are relative clauses that modify the nouns
**rrofá** *'dog'* and **njáu** *'cat':*

<div class="gloss">
  Tún rrofá jár on tún njáu vól wed jadé.<br/>
  tún rrofá jár on tún njáu vól wed ja-dé<br/>
  SEN dog eat\AN and.PRED SEN cat drink\AN soup see-1>3<br/>
  ! 'I see the soup that the dog is eating and the cat is drinking.'
</div>
