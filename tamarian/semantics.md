This page describes aspects of Tamarian semantics that are not already described elsewhere.

## Archetypes
Many metaphors feature the same set of characters, like Uzani, in different situations. Even if a Tamarian speaker does not know the origin behind the metaphors, it would not be too difficult to guess what kind of character Uzani is:

* **Uzani, his army with fists closed**: *'To confront, attack'*
* **Uzani, his army with fists open**: *'To spread out, to lure'*

Given just these two, Uzani is probably a warrior, a leader, and a tactician. While it wouldn't be possible to deduce the precise meaning of a completely unknown metaphor that happens to feature Uzani, one could still guess that it has something to do with fighting, leadership, or strategy.

Since no Tamarian speaker needs to care about who Uzani specifically is or what he has done, the name Uzani can simply be a stand-in, or Archetype, that represents warriors, leaders, and tacticians.

Archetypes used in many disparate metaphors represent broader or more abstract semantic categories: Darmok, whose metaphors involve being at sea, social isolation, and connection, could be the Archetype for sailors, travelers, people who are lonely, and people who seek friends.

## Idiom subversion
Most idioms have a canonical form: **Darmok and Jalad at Tanagra** always represents *'cooperation',* for instance. However, by replacing the metasubject with a different Archetype with a specific meaning, it is possible to create new, **subverted idioms** with somewhat compositional meaning, as long as the new idiom does not already exist .

For example, **Kimarnt, head cloudy** *'to drink alcohol'*, could be subverted by replacing usual Kimarnt with the warrior Uzani: **Uzani, head cloudy**, *'to be belligerently drunk'*.

The idea here is that, by subverting the normal metatopic with a new Archetype, the semantics of the new Archetype are imparted into the old idiom to create a new related meaning.
